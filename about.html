Overview

Attending UW-Madison for college, I started my time there in pursuit of a degree in Computer Science. I enjoyed the new challenge; neither I nor any of my family had done any kind of programming before. I also felt like I was suited to it, and I found joy in the work of making something functional where there once was nothing.

During my time in school, I took a grand tour through programming fundamentals in Java, architecture in C, graphics in Javascript, AI in Python, and more. I was also able to grow my experience by serving an internship at Subzero-Wolf group on their Website team. Not only did I run manual tests as a part of their agile cycle, but I also maintained and expanded their automated testing codebase with C# Selenium.

In my personal time, I have expanded my abilities by experimenting with website and game design, as well as taking online courses in SQL. On a seemingly different note, I've spent a lot of time in the last few years making art. My work has included drawing (pencil, charcoal, ink), painting (acrylic, oil, watercolor), and printmaking (relief, multi-block). This may not seem to fit the 'career overview' section. However, I believe that creativity is vital in devising solutions and making strong interpersonal connections, and is therefore indispensable in the workplace.

Volunteer Experience

Classroom Assistant

I spent two weeks serving in classrooms at Irving elementary school in West Allis, WI. This school's mission is to be inclusive to children of all backgrounds and cognitive ableness. It was very rewarding to get to know these students and find out exactly the best way to help their unique way of learning.

Scratch Club Leader

In this position, a few partners and I took the leadership role to run a club which would teach Scratch programming to 4th and 5th grade students. They say that you only truly understand something when you can explain it simply. Trying to explain programming concepts to these younger kids put that to the test.

Violence Prevention Team Member

While I was a member of the UW Badger Marching Band, I served on the team which educated new recruits as well as returning members on strategies for bystander intervention, recognizing intimate partner violence, and more. We would design workshops and presentations, and we would moderate small-group discussions to raise awareness and make sure that our community was a safe one.
